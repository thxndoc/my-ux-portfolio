NB: This was my first case study as part of my UX Design course in a program called Entry Level.

Abstract

We were given a task to create a mobile app that lets solo travelers connect with others. This report was created to better understand the process that users go through to connect with others, the specific problems faced by them, and to find a possible solution to that problem.

Role
UX Design. I was part of a program called 'Entry Level' where I was in a group of other UX Designers where we were each tasked in creating a mobile app.

Approach
Most of the research focused on interviews with the development of a persona which was then followed by a mobile app solution for solo traveller's.

Methods
Interviews, affinity mapping, persona, lightning demo, brainstorming, prototyping, usability testing.
Duration
6 weeks

User Interviews

I used interviews as my main method of research and created an affinity map to categorize the insights so that I could start building the persona.

Background

Mbali is a young professional who likes to travel. She also makes a small income from content creation related to travelling. She enjoys hiking or jogging on weekends. She likes trying out new places and "hidden gems" based on recommendations from people on social media sites like Tiktok and Instagram. In addition to that, she likes meeting new people and would like to connect with others because she believes there is something to learn from everybody.

Problem Statement

Solo travel has a way of opening up your mind to new experiences, makes you independent and gives you a chance to connect with new people. As a solo traveler, I want to feel well acquainted, secure and within budget so that I can travel stress-free. How might we provide our users with the tools to make their solo travel experiences the best they can be?

Brainstorming ideas

Budget planner app to prepare for your trip and track your spending. Includes money saving tips from others.

Travel planner mobile app including community tab for information about places to visit, things to do etc.

Platform to travel with others in a group, where the leader/guide shares information, alerts travelers about important times.

Platform where travelers meet in common area, can split for a period of time to do own thing, everyone's location is tracked for safety (only visible to guide) group meets back at common place.

App offering experiences in a group setting, allowing for people to meet each other.

Meet and greet style app. Anyone travelling to the same place can connect on the app and then meet and greet in person at the destination.

App with discounts on travel related things like flights and accommodation. Includes tips on how to prepare for your flight and making the most out of your stay.

App with "hidden gem" types of places (accommodation, restaurants, activities) recommended by others with reviews and ratings.

App focused on trying something new with someone and sharing your experience.

Chat style app where you can get real time advice from people who have been/on the same trip as you, ratings on places, do's and dont's (community watch style), updated regularly.

After brainstorming, I decided to go with the chat style app where solo travelers can get real-time advice from people who are on or have been on the trip they wish to go on. This would include travel tips, pictures, ratings on places and would be update regularly by the users. This would provide a way for people to connect with each other while also getting advice.

Usability testing

For the test, I created a high fidelity prototype and I invited 6 users to test the app using Figma and voice call. The feedback I received in terms of the ease of use, ease of interface and satisfaction of the app was app was good, although most of the users wanted an option to login with Google or Facebook for convenience.

When conducting the usability testing, I realised that I would have to add an option to sign in with Gmail or Facebook as this was more convenient for them to avoid having to remember another password.

Conclusion
