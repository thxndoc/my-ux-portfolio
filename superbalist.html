How a confirmation can improve the user experience

The shoe

I was on the Superbalist app and I navigated to my wishlist page to see if the shoe I had added was on sale...

The icon

I then scrolled down to find that this shoe was sold out and next to it was an icon that I didn't really recognize. I clicked on it and all of a sudden my shoe was gone! It disappeared in the blink of an eye. Below is a video of what happened...

click to play & click to pause

watch out for the pink shoe in the video

I was confused and wondered where the shoe had gone, so I searched until eventually I found it in my account section under 'waitlist'. Now I understood what the icon meant.

How might we improve the experience?

An acknowledgement put in a snackbar would improve my experience and alleviate my anxiety and confusion because I would know exactly where the shoe is. This would also help with recognition rather than recall.The user would receive confirmation that the item has been added to the waitlist (recognition), instead of hiding it immediately (recall).Let's look at the pink coin purse below as an example...

1. Pink coin purse is sold out

2. I click on the waitlist icon

3. Snackbar with acknowledgement appears

Taking it a step further...

Now that I know that my item has been moved to my 'waitlist' section, how can I improve the experience of getting to my waitlist?

This is the current user flow:

1. Click on hamburger menu

2. Click on 'Account'

3. Click on 'Waitlist'

How it can be improved

Adding a waitlist icon and text in the hamburger menu would make the experience easier.

Why?

For the user to recognize that the icon seen in the 'wishlist' section means that it is a 'waitlist' and to optimize the user experience by reducing the number of steps that need to be taken to get to the 'waitlist'.
